CHEMISTRY – 45 questions (180 marks)
Section A – Physical Chemistry (class XI–XII)
Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry
· Mole concept, molar mass, percentage composition, empirical & molecular formula, stoichiometry, limiting reagent
Structure of Atom
· Atomic number, isotopes, Bohr model, quantum numbers, orbitals, Aufbau, Pauli, Hund
Classification of Elements & Periodicity
· Modern periodic law, trends (atomic/ionic radii, ionisation enthalpy, electron gain, electronegativity, valency)
Chemical Bonding & Molecular Structure
· Ionic & covalent bonds, Lewis, VSEPR, valence-bond, hybridisation (sp, sp², sp³), molecular orbital theory (homonuclear diatomic), hydrogen bond
States of Matter – Gases & Liquids
· Ideal gas equation, kinetic theory, deviation (van der Waals), liquefaction, critical temperature, surface tension, viscosity
Thermodynamics
· System & surroundings, internal energy, enthalpy, Hess law, bond enthalpy, entropy, Gibbs energy, spontaneity
Equilibrium
· Dynamic equilibrium, Le Chatelier, ionic equilibrium, ionisation of acids & bases, pH, buffer, solubility product, common-ion effect
Redox Reactions
· Oxidation number, balancing (ion-electron & oxidation-number), electrochemical series
Solutions
· Concentration terms, Raoult’s law, ideal & non-ideal, colligative properties (elevation, depression, osmotic pressure), van’t Hoff factor
Electrochemistry
· Conductance, Kohlrausch, electrolytic & galvanic cells, electrode potential, Nernst equation, EMF, relationship with ΔG, batteries & fuel cells, corrosion
Chemical Kinetics
· Rate law, order & molecularity, integrated rate equations (0, 1, 2), half-life, Arrhenius, collision theory, catalysis
Surface Chemistry
· Adsorption (physisorption vs chemisorption), colloids (types, preparation, properties), emulsions, micelles
Section B – Inorganic Chemistry
13. Hydrogen
· Position in periodic table, isotopes, heavy water, hydrides
s-Block Elements (Group 1 & 2)
· General trends, anomalous properties of Li & Be, important ores, industrial preparation & uses of NaOH, Na₂CO₃, CaO, CaCO₃, cement
p-Block Elements
· Group 13 to 18 – important trends, allotropes (diamond, graphite, fullerene), oxides (acidic/basic/amphoteric), ammonia (Haber), nitric & phosphoric acids, ozone, sulphuric acid, interhalogens, noble gases
d- and f-Block Elements
· General introduction, electronic configuration, oxidation states, colour, magnetic properties, lanthanoid contraction, preparation & properties of KMnO₄ & K₂Cr₂O₇
Coordination Compounds
· Werner theory, ligands, coordination number, IUPAC nomenclature, isomerism, bonding (VBT & CFT briefly), importance in qualitative analysis, haemoglobin, chlorophyll, cis-platin
Environmental Chemistry
· Air, water & soil pollution, acid rain, ozone hole, greenhouse effect, green chemistry
Section C – Organic Chemistry
19. Purification & Characterisation
· Qualitative (Lassaigne, detection of N, S, halogens), quantitative (Carius, Kjeldahl), chromatography, distillation, crystallisation
Some Basic Principles & Techniques
· Tetravalence, shapes, homologous series, IUPAC, electronic effects (inductive, resonance, hyperconjugation), types of reactions (substitution, addition, elimination, rearrangement)
Hydrocarbons
· Alkanes (conformations, combustion, halogenation), alkenes (geometrical isomerism, Markovnikov, peroxide effect), alkynes (acidity, reduction), aromatic (Hückel, directive influence, carcinogenicity)
Haloalkanes & Haloarenes
· Nomenclature, nature of C–X bond, SN1/SN2/E1/E2, Grignard, polyhalogen compounds (freons, DDT)
Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers
· Methods of preparation, physical & chemical properties, dehydration, oxidation, Reimer–Tiemann, Williamson synthesis
Aldehydes, Ketones & Carboxylic Acids
· Nucleophilic addition, oxidation, reduction, aldol, Cannizzaro, HVZ, esterification, amide formation
Amines
· Structure, basicity, preparation, diazotisation, importance in sulpha drugs
Biomolecules
· Carbohydrates (classification, glycosidic linkage), proteins (α-helix, β-sheet, denaturation), vitamins (classification), nucleic acids (DNA vs RNA), hormones (basic idea)
Polymers
· Addition & condensation, copolymerisation, biodegradable polymers, PHBV, Nylon 6, 6-6, terylene, Buna-S, PVC
Chemistry in Everyday Life
· Drugs & classification (antacids, antihistamines, tranquilisers, analgesics, antimicrobials, antibiotics, antifertility), cleansing agents (soaps vs detergents), food preservatives, artificial sweeteners
BIOLOGY – 90 questions (360 marks)
Class XI (5 units)
Unit 1 – Diversity of Living Organisms
· Three domains of life, taxonomy & systematics, five-kingdom classification, salient features of Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia
· Viruses, viroids, lichens
· Botanical gardens, herbaria, zoological parks
· Classification of plants (Algae, Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms) – morphology & life cycles
· Classification of animals (non-chordates up to phyla, chordates up to classes) – diagnostic features & examples
Unit 2 – Structural Organisation in Plants & Animals
· Morphology of flowering plants – root, stem, leaf, inflorescence, flower, fruit, seed, semi-technical description of families (Solanaceae, Fabaceae, Liliaceae)
· Anatomy of flowering plants – tissue systems, secondary growth (stem & root)
· Structural organisation in animals – animal tissues, cockroach morphology & anatomy
Unit 3 – Cell: Structure & Functions
· Cell theory, prokaryotic vs eukaryotic, cell envelope, cell membrane fluid-mosaic model, membrane transport, cell wall
· Cell organelles – structure & functions (ER, Golgi, lysosomes, mitochondria, plastids, ribosomes, cytoskeleton, cilia, flagella, centrioles, nucleus)
· Biomolecules – chemical constituents (carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, nucleic acids), enzymes (properties, nomenclature, mechanism, factors affecting activity)
· Cell cycle – phases, mitosis, meiosis, significance
Unit 4 – Plant Physiology
· Transport – diffusion, facilitated diffusion, active transport, plant-water relations (imbibition, plasmolysis, osmosis, water potential), transpiration, uptake & transport of mineral nutrients, phloem transport (mass-flow hypothesis)
· Mineral nutrition – essential elements, macro vs micro, deficiency symptoms, nitrogen metabolism (biological nitrogen fixation)
· Photosynthesis – pigments, light & dark reactions, cyclic & non-cyclic photophosphorylation, chemiosmotic hypothesis, photorespiration, C₃ & C₄ pathways, CAM plants, factors affecting
· Respiration – glycolysis, fermentation, TCA cycle, ETS, oxidative phosphorylation, respiratory quotient
· Plant growth & development – phases, growth rates, conditions for growth, differentiation, dedifferentiation, redifferentiation, plant hormones (auxins, gibberellins, cytokinins, ethylene, ABA), seed dormancy, photoperiodism, vernalisation
Unit 5 – Human Physiology
· Digestion & absorption – alimentary canal, digestive glands, role of enzymes, absorption of digested products, disorders (PEM, constipation, jaundice, vomiting, diarrhoea)
· Breathing & exchange of gases – respiratory organs, mechanism of breathing, regulation, transport of gases, disorders (asthma, emphysema, occupational disorders)
· Body fluids & circulation – blood, lymph, composition, clotting, heart structure, cardiac cycle, ECG, double circulation, regulation, disorders (hypertension, coronary artery disease, angina pectoris, heart failure)
· Excretory products & elimination – modes, human excretory system, urine formation, osmoregulation, regulation of kidney function, disorders (uraemia, renal failure, renal calculi, nephritis, dialysis)
· Locomotion & movement – types of movement, skeletal system, joints, disorders of muscular & skeletal system (myasthenia gravis, tetany, muscular dystrophy, arthritis, osteoporosis, gout)
· Neural control & coordination – neuron, nerves, impulse conduction, CNS, PNS, reflex action, sensory receptors, sense organs (eye, ear)
· Chemical coordination & integration – endocrine glands & hormones (hypothalamus, pituitary, pineal, thyroid, parathyroid, thymus, adrenal, pancreas, gonads), mechanism of hormone action, role of second messengers
Class XII (5 units)
Unit 6 – Reproduction
· Reproduction in organisms – asexual (binary fission, budding, fragmentation, spore formation, regeneration, vegetative propagation), sexual (events, significance)
· Sexual reproduction in flowering plants – structure of flower, pre-fertilisation events, double fertilisation, post-fertilisation events, seed development, apomixis, polyembryony
· Human reproduction – male & female reproductive systems, gametogenesis, menstrual cycle, fertilisation, implantation, pregnancy, parturition, lactation
· Reproductive health – need, contraception (natural, barrier, IUD, oral, injectable, implants, surgical), medical termination of pregnancy, sexually transmitted diseases, infertility, assisted reproductive technologies (IVF, ZIFT, GIFT, ICSI, AI)
Unit 7 – Genetics & Evolution
· Principles of inheritance & variation – Mendel’s laws, chromosome theory, deviations (incomplete dominance, co-dominance, multiple alleles, pleiotropy, polygenic inheritance, sex-linked, sex-influenced, sex-limited), chromosomal disorders (Down’s, Klinefelter’s, Turner’s)
· Molecular basis of inheritance – DNA & RNA (structure, packaging), replication, transcription, genetic code, translation, regulation of gene expression (lac operon), human genome project, DNA fingerprinting
· Evolution – origin of life, theories (special creation, abiogenesis, biogenesis), evidences, adaptive radiation, mechanisms (Lamarck, Darwin), Hardy–Weinberg principle, origin & evolution of man
Unit 8 – Biology in Human Welfare
· Human health & disease – common diseases (typhoid, pneumonia, common cold, malaria, amoebiasis, ascariasis, ringworm, AIDS, cancer, drugs & alcohol abuse)
· Strategies for enhancement in food production – animal husbandry (breeding, Bee-keeping, fisheries), plant breeding (green revolution, high-yielding varieties, disease resistance), tissue culture, single-cell protein, apiculture
· Microbes in human welfare – household products (curd, cheese, bread), industrial products (alcohol, acetic acid, antibiotics), enzymes, sewage treatment, biogas, biocontrol agents, biofertilisers
Unit 9 – Biotechnology & Its Applications
· Biotechnology – principles & processes – tools (restriction enzymes, cloning vectors, competent host), processes (isolation, fragmentation, ligation, transformation, selection), amplification (PCR), downstream processing
· Biotechnology & its applications – genetically modified organisms (Bt cotton, RNAi, insulin, gene therapy), transgenic animals, ethical issues, biosafety
Unit 10 – Ecology & Environment
· Organisms & populations – population attributes, population growth (exponential & logistic), life-history variations, population interactions (predation, competition, parasitism, commensalism, mutualism, amensalism)
· Ecosystem – structure, function, productivity, decomposition, energy flow (pyramids), ecological succession (hydrosere, xerosere), nutrient cycling (carbon, phosphorus), ecosystem services
· Biodiversity & conservation – levels, patterns, importance, loss, conservation (in-situ & ex-situ), hotspots, endangered organisms, red data book, sacred groves
· Environmental issues – air pollution (smog, ozone depletion, acid rain), water pollution (eutrophication), solid waste (green chemistry, integrated waste management), radioactive waste, greenhouse effect & global warming, climate change, deforestation, case studies (Chipko, Silent Valley), environmental laws
